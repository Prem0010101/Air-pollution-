About Air Pollution

Air pollution refers to the contamination of the atmosphere by harmful substances such as gases, smoke, dust, and chemicals. It poses a serious risk to human health, ecosystems, and the planet’s climate.

According to the WHO, air pollution kills over 7 million people every year. It is one of the world’s largest environmental health risks.

Main Causes

Vehicles

Exhaust from cars and trucks releases carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter into the air.

Industries

Factories emit greenhouse gases and pollutants that harm both the environment and human health.

Fossil Fuels

Burning coal, oil, and gas produces harmful emissions like sulfur dioxide and carbon dioxide.

Deforestation

Trees absorb carbon dioxide. Cutting them down increases CO₂ levels and reduces air quality.

Effects of Air Pollution

Health Risks

Causes asthma, lung cancer, heart disease, and reduces overall life expectancy.

Climate Change

Pollutants like CO₂ and methane trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming.

Damage to Nature

Acid rain and smog damage crops, forests, and aquatic ecosystems.

Solutions

Use Public Transport

Fewer cars on the road mean less emissions and cleaner air.

Switch to Renewable Energy

Solar, wind, and hydro energy reduce reliance on fossil fuels.

Plant More Trees

Trees act as natural air filters by absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen.
