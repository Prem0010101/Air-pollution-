About Air Pollution

Air pollution refers to harmful substances in the atmosphere, such as gases, chemicals, smoke, and particulate matter. It impacts both the environment and human health.

Main Causes

Vehicles

Emits nitrogen oxides, carbon monoxide, and particulate matter.

Industries

Factories release harmful pollutants into the atmosphere.

Fossil Fuels

Burning coal, oil, and gas increases toxic emissions.

Deforestation

Cutting trees reduces CO₂ absorption and worsens air quality.

Effects of Air Pollution

Health Risks

Asthma, lung cancer, heart disease, and reduced lifespan.

Climate Change

Greenhouse gases trap heat and warm the planet.

Damage to Nature

Forests, crops, and ecosystems are severely harmed.

Solutions

Use Public Transport

Reduces traffic emissions and improves air quality.

Switch to Renewable Energy

Solar and wind energy reduce dependence on fossil fuels.

Plant More Trees

Trees absorb CO₂ and release clean oxygen.
